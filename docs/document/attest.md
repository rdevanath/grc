# Attestation Standard vs Reporting Standard

| Category            | Attestation Standard                                                                 | Reporting Standard                                                                 |
|---------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Definition**       | A framework that governs how a practitioner (e.g., auditor) performs an attestation engagement — where they evaluate and provide assurance on a subject matter based on someone else’s assertion. | A framework that governs how the results of an engagement are communicated — including format, content, and required disclosures in the final report. |
| **Example**          | SSAE 18 is an attestation standard used for SOC 1 & SOC 2 reports.                  | The structure of a SOC 1 & SOC 2 report (sections like management assertion, system description, auditor’s opinion). |
| **Focus**            | Methodology, independence, evidence gathering, and assurance level.                 | Presentation, clarity, and completeness of the report.                             |
| **Used For**         | SOC reports, financial forecasts, compliance reviews, etc.                          | Audit reports, SOC reports, financial statements, etc.                             |
