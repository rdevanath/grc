    NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0 - SP 800-53

    Introduction

    The NIST CSF 2.0 is a voluntary, risk-based framework designed by the U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology to help organizations of any size manage and reduce cybersecurity risk.

    Core to CSF 2.0 are six functions that form a continuous improvement lifecycle:

    • Govern: Establish governance policies, roles, and oversight (new in 2.0).

    • Identify: Inventory assets, understand business context, and assess risk.

    • Protect: Implement safeguards such as access control, training, and data security.

    • Detect: Continuously monitor for anomalies and cybersecurity events.

    • Respond: Plan and execute actions to contain and mitigate incidents.

    • Recover: Restore systems and services while incorporating lessons learned.

    Implementation Tiers: These describe the maturity of an organization's cybersecurity risk management, ranging from Partial (Tier 1) to Adaptive (Tier 4), indicating a progression from less formal to more proactive approaches.

    NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5.1.1 controls

    The most comprehensive and standardized catalog of security and privacy controls (it defines controls – not just policies) used across U.S. federal agencies, critical infrastructure, and increasingly by private enterprises for robust cybersecurity governance.

    Full name: Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems and Organizations

    What Are NIST SP 800-53 Controls?

    • Developed by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST).

    • Provides a catalog of 400+ controls for securing information systems and protecting privacy.

    • Used to implement FISMA, FedRAMP, and align with NIST CSF, ISO 27001, and other frameworks.

    • Controls are grouped into 20 families, each addressing a specific security domain.

    Control Baselines - NIST defines three impact levels:

    • Low: ~149 controls

    • Moderate: ~287 controls

    • High: ~370 controls

    These baselines help tailor control selection based on system sensitivity and risk exposure.

    Control Structure - Each control includes:

    • Control statement: What must be done (e.g., “The organization must manage user accounts.”).

    • Control enhancements: Optional add-ons for higher assurance.

    • Discussion: Rationale and examples.

    • Implementation guidance: How to apply it in real-world systems.

    What’s New in Rev. 5.1.1 (2023–2025)

    • Rev. 5 (latest version) integrates privacy controls alongside security — making it suitable for GDPR, HIPAA, and other privacy regimes.

    • New controls for identity providers, cryptographic key protection, and token management.

    • Enhanced mappings to NIST CSF 2.0, ISO/IEC 27001:2022, and the NIST Privacy Framework.

    • Leading zeros added to control IDs (e.g., AC-01 instead of AC-1) for consistency.
